Common Mental Health Conditions

A brief overview of key mental health issues and how they may present.

Depression

  • Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, appetite/sleep changes.
  • Impact: Strained relationships, reduced performance at school/work.
  • Risk factors: Genetics, trauma, major life changes.
  • Treatment: Therapy (CBT, IPT), antidepressant medication, lifestyle changes.

Anxiety

  • Symptoms: Excessive worry, restlessness, irritability, rapid heartbeat.
  • Types: GAD, Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder.
  • Triggers: Stressful events, environment, genetics.
  • Coping: Mindfulness, relaxation techniques, CBT.

Substance Use Disorders

  • Substances: Alcohol, opioids, stimulants, prescriptions.
  • Consequences: Legal/health issues, strained relationships.
  • Co‑occurring: Often alongside other mental health issues.
  • Treatment: Detox, counselling, support groups (e.g., AA), MAT.

Psychosis (e.g., Schizophrenia)

  • Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, impaired functioning.
  • Onset: Often late adolescence/early adulthood.
  • Stigma: Common misunderstanding and fear.
  • Management: Antipsychotics, therapy, community support.

Post‑Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

  • Symptoms: Flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, avoidance.
  • Triggers: Past trauma, especially from violence or abuse.
  • Impact: Challenges in relationships, school, or work.
  • Treatment: Trauma‑focused therapy (e.g., EMDR), medication, support networks.
